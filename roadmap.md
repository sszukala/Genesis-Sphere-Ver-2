## üîÅ Reverse-Engineered Formulas for Cyclic Cosmology

After identifying strong alignment between the Genesis-Sphere framework and cyclic cosmological models (e.g., Steinhardt‚ÄìTurok, Tolman), we reverse-engineered the original functions to better fit oscillatory, time-symmetric, and bounded universe behavior.

---

### ‚úÖ Key Observations

- The original $\omega$-based sinusoidal projection already maps well to cosmic cycles.
- The original $D(t) = 1 + \alpha t^2$ and $Tf(t) = \frac{1}{1 + \beta(|t| + \epsilon)}$ were unbounded or monotonic ‚Äî not ideal for modeling repeating or bouncing universes.
- We refactored both to support **recurrence**, **bounded growth**, and **cyclic temporal modulation**.

---

### üìê Revised Functions

#### üß≠ Sinusoidal Projection Function (unchanged):

$$

$$

> Still effective for modeling oscillatory projection and smooth time-space modulation.

#### üîÅ Modified Dimension Expansion Function:

$$
D_{\text{cyc}}(t) = \frac{1 + \alpha t^2}{1 + \gamma t^4}
$$

> Bounded, symmetric, and avoids runaway inflation. Models entropy or spatial degrees of freedom with damping.

---

#### üîÆ Modified Temporal Flow Function:

$$
Tf_{\text{cyc}}(t) = \frac{\cos^2(\omega t)}{1 + \beta t^2}
$$

> Oscillatory and bounded. Reflects recurring time distortions instead of one-time slowdowns.

#### üß™ New Time-Density Function:

$$
\rho_{\text{cyc}}(t) = S(t) \cdot D_{\text{cyc}}(t)

> Naturally produces smooth, recurring density patterns ‚Äî ideal for simulating cycles or "bounces" in cosmological time.
